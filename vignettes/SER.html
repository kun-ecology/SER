SER-vignette

This tutorial is about how to calculate short-period environmental regime (SER) with a R package SER. It provides basic information of how short-period environmental rigime is developed and how it can be used for hydrological data as an example. It includes following parts:

  • Overview: introduction about how these indices are constructed.
  • Arguments of the SER function: detail information about three arguments of the main function SER.
  • Example: an example that shows how SER apply to hydrological data.
  • Potential application of this function: apart from short-period hydrological indices, its potential usage is discussed briefly.

Overview

Environment regime (or environment pattern), unlike traditional environmental variables, is defined as the environmental dynamic characteristics during a given (either long or short) period, such as change rate, distribution and extreme events, which might be masked by using simple average or median values. As linking environmental changes to biotic responses is one of the central topics in ecology, incorporating environment regimes into ecological studies can greatly advance our understanding between biota-environment relations. For example, short-period flow regime indices (e.g., skewness, change rate in flow events) played a vital role in riverine algal community (Qu et al. 2019; Wu et al. 2018, 2019)

With the facilitation of science and technology, more and more high frequently (daily or even hourly) measured environmental variables (e.g., nutrient concentration, dissolved oxygen) are available nowadays. These high frequency measured data allow computing environment regime indices. However, an R package or function that can be easily used to calculate short-term environment regime is still missing.

Therefore, we developed the so called short-period environmental regime (SER) to characterize such frequently measured environmental data and make them suitable to link with spot shot biotic data. Such regime is inspired by Olden and Poff (2003), which developed a series of indices to identify the characteristics of flow variation in riverine study. In total, 11 elementary indices that concentrate more about environmental variation in short-period were developed (Table 1). It should be noted that short-period defined here is case specific. For example, for some study, changes in days are the interested period, while for other, hours/months/years may be the target time interval. For current version, this function is developed with regarding days as the interested period, since we do not have available data that interested in other time interval.

Table 1. Detail information of the 11 elementary indices

Code Unit Definition
Magnitude of environmental data
MA1 Depending on types of environmental data Mean of the daily environmental data in N days before sampling date.
MA2 Depending on types of environmental data Median of the daily environmental data in N days before sampling date
MA3 % Coefficient of variation in N days before sampling date
MA4 unitless Skewness of N days environmental data
Frequency of environmental data
ML1 days Low pulse count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of environmental regimes below a lower threshold. Low pulse is defined as the number of days in which the environmental data drops below the 25th percentile of all daily values for the giving time period.
MH1 days High pulse count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of environmental regimes below a higher threshold. High pulse is defined as the number of days in which the environmental data above the 75th percentile of all daily values for the giving time period.
EL1 days Extreme low pulse count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of environmental regimes remains below a extreme lower threshold. Extreme low pulse is defined as the number of days in which the environmental data drops below the 10th percentile of all daily values for the time period.
EH1 days Extreme high pulse count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of environmental remains above a extreme higher threshold. Extreme high pulse is defined as the number of days in which the environmental data above the 90th percentile of all daily values for the time period.
Rate of change of environmental data
RC Depending on types of environmental data Rate of change in N days before sampling date.
RH1 days Numbers of day change rises in N days before sampling date. i.e., numbers of positive change
RL1 days Numbers of day change decline in N days before sampling date. i.e., numbers of negative change
* Units of some indices are dependent on types of environmental data.
† Currently, the function is developed with regarding days as the interested period, more features are under development.
‡ N can be defined as any interested time period, by default N=BetwSamT (days between two successive sampling dates).

Arguments of the SER function

  • Env_data: time series environmental data, a dataframe with columns Date and Env, make sure Date is in date format while Env is in numeric format.

  • sample_date: sampling dates, a vector containing sampling days. Date for initiation of the experiment (measurements) should be included as the first element of the vector.

  • days_bf: N days before the sampling date (not included); by default, days_bf = c(days between two successive sampling dates)

Examples

Below is an example showing how short-period environmental regime can be used for discharge data. Firstly, short-period environmental regime (in this case it is short-period hydrological regime) in days between two successive sampling dates (Table 2) were developed based on the 11 elementary indices. In total, 11 short-period hydrological indices were developed. The indices’ names were constructed as the combination of short time period and names of elementary indices, for example, BetwSamT.MA1 and BetwSamT.RC stand for mean of the daily average flow and mean rate of change in days between two successive sampling days respectively.

Table 2. Short-period hydrological indices

Code Unit Definition
Magnitude of flow events
MA1 L/s Mean of the daily average flow values in N days before sampling date
MA2 L/s Median of the daily average flow values in N days before sampling date
MA3 % Coefficient of variation in N days before sampling date
MA4 Skewness of N days flows
Frequency of flow events
ML1 days Low flood pulse count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of flow remains below a lower threshold. Low flow pulse is defined as the number of days in which the flow drops below the 25th percentile of all daily values for the time period (2016 - 2018).
MH1 days Number of occurrences during N days which the magnitude of flow remains above a higher threshold. High flood pulses are defined as the number of days in which the flow rises above the 75th percentile (high pulse) of all daily values for the time period (2016 - 2018).
EL1 days Extreme low flow count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of flow remains below a lower threshold. Extreme low flow pulse is defined as the number of days in which the flow drops below the 10th percentile of all daily values for the time period (2016 - 2018).
EH1 days Extreme high flow count N days: numbers of occurrence during N days which the magnitude of flow remains above a higher threshold. Extreme high flow pulse is defined as the number of days in which the flow above the 90th percentile of all daily values for the time period (2016 - 2018)
Rate of change of flow events
RC L/s Mean rate of change in N days before sampling date.
RH1 days Numbers of day change rises in N days before sampling date. i.e., numbers of positive flow change
RL1 days Numbers of day change decline in N days before sampling date. i.e., numbers of negative flow change

It should be noted that the time period for estimating high/low flow pulse is 2016-2018, which is the time period interested in the unpublished work. When applying function with other types of data, such time period is automatically defined as the period of the input data Env_data.

There are two data sets:

  • hydro_df is a dataframe, i.e., discharge data for three streams for the period of 2016-2018. There are two columns, i.e., Date (should be in date format) and Discharge (should be numeric data; unit: L/s). Below is the structure of the first elements in the list.

Potential application of the indices

  • Here we only used stream discharge data to estimate short-period hydrological regime, but this function is also suitable for estimation of other types of environmental data e.g., nutrient concentration, light, dissolved oxygen and etc.

  • In addition, by changing the argument days_bf, it is possible to get regimes in any interested period, such as between hours, months or years, as long as the data is measured in corresponding manners.

  • More features are under development.
